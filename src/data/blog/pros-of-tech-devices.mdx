## The Device Revolution: Context & Relevance

In 2026 a device is far more than consumer
electronics. It represents an edge processing
node — a bridge between physical effort and
digital scalability. The shift toward a
device-centric society is driven by the need
for low-latency decision-making and the
decentralization of intelligence.

## Root Causes of Device Proliferation

The Collapse of Computing Costs: As silicon
fabrication hit the 2nm milestone, the cost per
transistor plummeted — making it feasible to
embed logic into everything from industrial
valves to kitchen appliances.

Global Data Accessibility: The maturation of 5G
and LEO satellite constellations made devices
always-on by default, removing the final barrier
between physical and digital interaction.

## Competitive Advantages

Processing Power: Zero-lag local AI execution
due to NPU miniaturization built into modern
chips. Portability: Global mobility enabled by
solid-state battery improvements.

## Performance Optimization

Monitor thermal baseline — keep temperatures
below 85°C to avoid electromigration damage.
Maintain battery charge cycles between 20-80%
to preserve long-term battery health.
