## The Significance of Holi

Holi commemorates the legend of Prahlad and Holika.
When Hiranyakashipu tried to burn his son Prahlad,
Prahlad emerged unscathed while Holika perished.
This is why we celebrate Holika Dahan — the sacred
bonfire lit the night before the colors come out.

In Mathura and Vrindavan, Holi festivities last over
a week. The famous Lathmar Holi of Barsana is over
400 years old.

## How to Celebrate Holi Safely

Go Organic: Use natural herbal colors made from
turmeric, marigolds, or beetroot. Apply coconut oil
to hair and skin before stepping out. Traditional
delicacies include Gujiya, Thandai, and Dahi Vada.

## Dussehra: Victory of Truth

Dussehra symbolizes the victory of Dharma over
Adharma, celebrated on the tenth day of Ashvin.
In Northern India it celebrates Lord Rama defeating
Ravana. In Eastern India it marks Goddess Durga
slaying Mahishasura after a nine-day battle.

Ramlila performances re-enact Lord Rama's life over
multiple nights, culminating in the burning of giant
effigies of Ravana, Meghnada, and Kumbhakarna.
